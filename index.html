About Me

I am a firm believer that everything matters, but not everything is necessary (EMNEIN). In other words, every single occurrence, action, behavior, and thought is significant, but its significance does not suggest it was appropriate, optimal, or good. Because everything matters, I also believe in listening more than I speak--even to those who are wrong. There is just as much to be learned from what is wrong as there is from what is good. Thus, EMNEIN. For one to believe that everything matters, they must have great respect for everyone and everything they encounter. They must be curious in nature and desire to uncover absolute truth. To discover what is both significant and optimal, one must not be afraid to change their thoughts, behaviors, and separate themselves from their identity and hubris.

My name is Griffin McKnight and I am a Senior Computational Mathematics student at Liberty University. I enjoy studying mathematics because it allows us to quantitatively describe every aspect of our universe and daily lives. Dedicated to a lifelong pursuit of knowledge, perspective, and truth, I am passionate about better understanding the natural world through the use of statistical analysis, data visualization, predictive modeling, and computer vision. With this perspective, it excites me to find a story behind a dataset and during the process, learning something along the way.

I like to spend late nights, and occasionally early mornings, working on predictive modeling, feature engineering, and developing computational solutions to problems in daily life. When my obsessive compulsiveness towards an idea fades, I enjoy spending my time hiking, learning about and caring for my plants, and watching YouTube videos about bushcraft, survival, math theory, and coding. I am interested in researching machine learning applications in transportation, agriculture, permaculture, defense and security, economics, cryptocurrency, psychology, and group theory.

Take a look at my resume and projects, connect with me on socials, and reach out if I can help you better use and understand the data in your life.
